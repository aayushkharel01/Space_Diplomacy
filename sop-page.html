What are SOPs?

The Mandatory Checklist That Saves Lives

Standard Operating Procedure Defined

A Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) is a detailed, step-by-step instruction set that government agencies use to ensure complex tasks are always performed correctly, consistently, and in the same order. Think of it as a pre-approved checklist that removes confusion and eliminates delay during high-pressure situations when every minute counts.

In everyday government operations, officials might debate the best course of action, seek multiple approvals, or wait for meetings to decide on resource allocation. But during disasters, this bureaucratic process costs lives.

An SOP pre-authorizes specific actions, so the moment a trigger event occurs, the entire response mechanism activates automatically‚Äîno questions asked, no waiting for permission.

The Space-Based SOP: Eliminating Data Latency

In a disaster scenario, we cannot afford to have officials waiting for emergency meetings to decide whether they should contact a space agency or request satellite imagery. A mandatory Space-Based SOP makes the use of satellite information automatic and immediate.

The primary goal of this system is to eliminate data latency‚Äîthe critical delay between when a satellite captures imagery and when processed, actionable information reaches rescue workers on the ground. In many past disasters, this delay stretched to days or even weeks. A mandatory SOP cuts this timeline from days to mere hours.

The Five-Step Workflow

The SOP operates through a clear five-step workflow that functions like a rapid-response machine:

1 Crisis Declaration
Official Disaster Alert

A designated government agency issues a formal declaration that a major event (earthquake, flood, cyclone) has occurred. This serves as the starting trigger.
