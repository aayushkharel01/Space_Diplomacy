The Mandate for Investment

The ultimate success of this campaign depends on translating awareness into concrete policy outcomes. We have the technology. We have the global frameworks. Now we need political will and investment to make Space-Based SOPs mandatory in every country.

Pillar 1: Legislative Action

Governments must pass legislation that makes Space-Based SOPs a legal requirement, not a discretionary option.

This Legislation Should Specify:

  • Automatic activation triggers (magnitude thresholds for earthquakes, rainfall thresholds for floods, wind speeds for cyclones)
  • Maximum acceptable timeframes for each step of the SOP workflow (with the three-hour standard as the target)
  • Designated responsible agencies and clear lines of authority during disasters
  • Mandatory annual drills and training exercises to maintain readiness
  • Penalties or accountability mechanisms for non-compliance during actual disasters

Why Legislation Matters:

Without legal mandates, satellite data remains optional. During crises, optional measures are often delayed or ignored. Making SOPs legally binding removes decision-making delays and ensures immediate action.

Pillar 2: Infrastructure Investment

Legislation alone is insufficient. Countries must invest in physical infrastructure and human capital to achieve true disaster sovereignty.

Essential Investments Include:

  • Establish national or regional Data Processing Centers equipped with high-performance computing systems
  • Train a dedicated cadre of remote sensing analysts, GIS specialists, and emergency coordinators
  • Develop or procure automated processing algorithms tailored to local geography and disaster types
  • Create redundant communication systems to ensure processed maps reach field teams even when standard networks fail
  • Consider investment in domestic satellite capabilities or regional consortia for countries with sufficient resources

The Sovereignty Argument:

Relying on foreign expertise during disasters creates dangerous dependencies. Nations must build their own capacity to interpret and act on satellite data independently. This isn't just about emergency response‚Äîit's about national security and sovereignty.

Pillar 3: International Cooperation

Even with domestic capacity, international cooperation remains vital for comprehensive disaster response.

Key Actions for Governments:

  • Join or strengthen participation in the International Charter "Space and Major Disasters"
  • Engage with UN-SPIDER for ongoing capacity building and knowledge exchange
  • Participate in bilateral or regional agreements for satellite data sharing
  • Contribute to global standards development for satellite-based disaster response protocols
  • Support space capacity building in neighboring or partner countries to create regional resilience networks

The Global Solidarity Principle:

Disasters don't respect borders. A strong nation helps its neighbors build capacity, creating regional resilience that benefits everyone. The International Charter proves global cooperation is possible‚Äînow we must expand and strengthen it.
